Chemistry: The Central Science

Chemistry is the branch of science that studies the composition, structure, and properties of matter. It connects physics with biology and helps explain how substances interact and change.

From understanding the air we breathe to the reactions that power our bodies, chemistry is everywhere. It plays a major role in developing new medicines, energy sources, and materials that make life easier.

Learning chemistry enhances problem-solving and critical-thinking skills—important abilities not only for scientists, but also for anyone pursuing a STEM career.

Common Elements and Their Uses

Element Common Applications
Everyday Use Industrial Use
Oxygen (O₂) Breathing and combustion Steel production, water treatment
Hydrogen (H₂) Fuel cells, ammonia production Rocket fuel, refining processes
Carbon (C) Pencils (graphite), diamonds Steel manufacturing, carbon fibers
Silicon (Si) Electronics, glass Semiconductors, solar panels
Sodium (Na) Table salt (NaCl) Chemical manufacturing, soap production
